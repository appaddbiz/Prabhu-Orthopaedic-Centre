Restoring Balance, Mobility & Comfort

Our toes, though small in size, play a significant role in maintaining balance, enabling movement, and bearing weight while walking or running. Deformities, injuries, or diseases affecting the toes can cause pain, hinder mobility, and reduce quality of life. Toes surgery is often the most effective solution when conservative treatments fail to relieve symptoms or correct structural abnormalities.

If you're dealing with persistent toe pain, deformities like bunions or hammer toes, or post-traumatic complications, exploring Toes Surgery under an expert like Dr. Chetan Patil S—known for offering the best Toes Surgery in Bangalore—can significantly improve your outcomes.

What is Toes Surgery?

Toes surgery refers to a variety of surgical procedures performed on the toes to correct deformities, repair injuries, alleviate chronic pain, or restore proper alignment and function. This branch of orthopedic foot surgery can involve soft tissue repair, bone realignment, joint reconstruction, or removal of growths depending on the condition.

Common reasons for toes surgery include:

  • Bunions (hallux valgus)
  • Hammer toe or claw toe
  • Toe fractures or dislocations
  • Arthritis in toe joints
  • Morton’s neuroma
  • Overlapping or curled toes
  • Ingrown toenails with recurring infections
  • Calluses or corns due to deformity
Types of Toe Surgeries

The choice of toe surgery depends on the diagnosis and severity of the problem. Common surgical procedures include:

  • Bunionectomy: This procedure corrects the bony bump on the big toe joint by removing excess bone and realigning the joint to reduce pain and restore function.
  • Hammer Toe Correction: For toes bent downward at the middle joint, surgery may involve tendon release, joint fusion, or implant insertion to straighten the toe.
  • Toe Joint Fusion (Arthrodesis): This surgery involves fusing the bones of a toe joint to treat severe arthritis, deformity, or instability, typically done in the big or smaller toes.
  • Osteotomy: A surgical cut and repositioning of bone to correct misalignment, often done as part of bunion or hammer toe surgery.
  • Tendon Transfers: This technique balances the pull of tendons by relocating one or more tendons to help straighten a deformed toe.
  • Exostectomy: Removal of bone spurs or growths causing pain and discomfort during movement.
  • Toe Fracture Repair: For displaced or unstable fractures, surgical fixation using wires, pins, or screws may be required to ensure proper healing.
Diagnosis and Evaluation

Before recommending surgery, a thorough diagnostic process is followed:

  • Clinical Examination – Assessing deformity, range of motion, gait, and areas of pain
  • X-rays or MRI Scans – To evaluate bone, joint, and soft tissue abnormalities
  • Foot Pressure Analysis – Used in complex cases to study foot mechanics
  • Non-Surgical Trial – Includes orthotics, splints, physical therapy, or medication

If conservative treatment fails to relieve symptoms or the condition worsens, toes surgery is recommended to restore alignment and function.

Recovery and Rehabilitation

Recovery after toes surgery depends on the complexity of the procedure, patient’s health status, and post-op compliance. Here's what to expect during each phase:

Immediate Post-Surgery (1–2 Weeks):
  • Rest, ice, compression, and elevation (RICE)
  • Limited movement with toe splints or surgical shoes
  • Pain control with medications
  • Wound care and dressing changes
Early Recovery (2–6 Weeks):
  • Gradual weight-bearing as per the surgeon’s guidance
  • Removal of stitches or pins (if used)
  • Start of gentle toe movements to prevent stiffness
Rehabilitation Phase (6–12 Weeks):
  • Physiotherapy to restore range of motion and strength
  • Footwear modifications or orthotics
  • Balance and gait retraining
Full Recovery:
  • Most patients return to normal activity in 8–12 weeks
  • Athletes or those with physically demanding routines may need 3–6 months

Adherence to post-operative instructions plays a crucial role in ensuring optimal recovery after the best toes surgery in Bangalore.

When Should You Consider Toes Surgery?

You should consult a foot and ankle specialist if you notice:

  • Constant pain in your toes or forefoot
  • Difficulty wearing regular shoes
  • Deformity of one or more toes
  • Pain that limits walking, running, or standing
  • Recurrent infections or ulcers due to toe deformity

Delaying treatment may lead to worsening of the condition or permanent disability. Early consultation leads to better outcomes and faster recovery.
