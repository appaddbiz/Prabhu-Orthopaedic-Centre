PRP Injections (Platelet-Rich Plasma): Natural Healing for Joints and Soft Tissues

Chronic joint pain, tendon injuries, and degenerative conditions can significantly impact your mobility and quality of life. What are PRP Injections?

Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP) is a regenerative therapy derived from a patient’s own blood. It contains a high concentration of platelets that are rich in growth factors—biological agents that stimulate healing in injured tissues.

How it Works:

  • Blood is drawn from the patient and placed in a centrifuge.
  • The centrifuge spins the blood to separate the plasma and concentrate the platelets.
  • This Platelet-Rich Plasma is then injected directly into the injured area—such as a joint, tendon, or ligament—using ultrasound or fluoroscopic guidance.
  • The injected PRP enhances the body’s natural ability to heal itself by reducing inflammation, improving tissue repair, and stimulating cell growth.
Conditions Treated with PRP Injections
  • ✅ Knee osteoarthritis
  • ✅ Tennis elbow or golfer’s elbow
  • ✅ Rotator cuff injuries
  • ✅ Achilles tendonitis
  • ✅ Plantar fasciitis
  • ✅ Meniscus injuries
  • ✅ Ligament sprains and strains
  • ✅ Cartilage injuries
  • ✅ Post-surgical healing enhancement

At Prabhu Orthopaedic Centre, we use PRP therapy as both a standalone treatment and an adjunct to surgical and non-surgical orthopaedic care.

Benefits of PRP Therapy
  • ✔️ Minimally invasive – No incisions, stitches, or long hospital stays
  • ✔️ Reduced inflammation – Promotes anti-inflammatory healing from within
  • ✔️ Natural and safe – Uses your own blood, reducing allergic or adverse reactions
  • ✔️ Faster recovery – Speeds up tissue regeneration and pain relief
  • ✔️ Delay or avoid surgery – Ideal for early-stage degenerative conditions or sports injuries
Why Choose PRP Over Steroid Injections?

While steroid injections offer short-term relief, they do not heal the underlying problem and may cause side effects with repeated use. PRP, on the other hand, stimulates true healing and tissue regeneration.

Dr. Chetan Patil S, the Best Orthopaedic Doctor in Bangalore, often recommends PRP to athletes, active individuals, and patients with chronic joint pain who want a sustainable, long-term solution.

PRP Injection Procedure at Prabhu Orthopaedic Centre

1. Initial Consultation and Diagnosis

  • A thorough physical exam, patient history, and imaging (X-rays, MRI, or ultrasound)
  • Evaluation of whether PRP therapy is suitable for your specific condition

2. Blood Collection

  • Approximately 15–30 ml of blood is drawn from the arm

3. Centrifugation

  • The blood is processed in a high-speed centrifuge to separate and concentrate the platelets

4. Injection

  • The PRP is injected directly into the affected joint or tissue using sterile technique and often under ultrasound guidance for precision

5. Post-Procedure Care

  • Minimal downtime; patients may resume light activities within 1–2 days
  • Avoid anti-inflammatory medications (like ibuprofen) for a few days, as they can interfere with the healing process
  • A tailored physiotherapy program may be advised for optimal results
Expected Results and Recovery Timeline

Patients typically notice gradual improvements over 2 to 6 weeks. Pain relief, improved joint function, and reduction in inflammation continue for several months as the tissues regenerate.

Number of Sessions:

  • 1 to 3 injections spaced several weeks apart may be recommended, depending on the severity and location of the condition.
PRP vs. Surgery

In many cases, PRP therapy can delay or even eliminate the need for surgery, especially in early-stage osteoarthritis, mild cartilage defects, or tendon injuries. When surgery is unavoidable, PRP can also be used post-operatively to enhance healing and reduce recovery time.
