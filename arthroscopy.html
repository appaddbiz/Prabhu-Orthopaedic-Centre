A Minimally Invasive Solution for Joint Problems

Joint pain, swelling, or limited movement can significantly affect your daily life. While many joint issues can be managed with medication or physiotherapy, some require surgical intervention. That’s where Arthroscopy comes in—a minimally invasive procedure that allows orthopedic surgeons to diagnose and treat joint conditions effectively with less pain, faster healing, and minimal scarring.

If you're looking for advanced orthopedic care, consult Dr. Chetan Patil S, who is widely regarded for offering the best Arthroscopy in Bangalore.

What is Arthroscopy?

Arthroscopy is a minimally invasive surgical technique used to diagnose and treat problems inside a joint. The procedure involves inserting a small camera (arthroscope) through a tiny incision to view the joint on a monitor. Special surgical instruments are then used through other small incisions to perform the required treatment.

Unlike traditional open surgery, arthroscopy offers faster recovery, reduced tissue damage, minimal scars, and lower complication risks.

Joints Commonly Treated with Arthroscopy:
  • Knee
  • Shoulder
  • Hip
  • Elbow
  • Ankle
  • Wrist
Conditions Treated with Arthroscopy

Knee:

  • Torn meniscus
  • ACL (anterior cruciate ligament) tears
  • Cartilage injuries
  • Synovitis (inflammation of the joint lining)
  • Loose bone or cartilage fragments

Shoulder:

  • Rotator cuff tears
  • Labral tears
  • Shoulder impingement syndrome
  • Frozen shoulder
  • Recurrent dislocations

Hip:

  • Labral tears
  • Femoroacetabular impingement (FAI)
  • Synovitis
  • Loose bodies

Other Joints:

  • Elbow arthritis
  • Ankle instability or impingement
  • Wrist ganglion cysts or ligament injuries

Arthroscopy is both a diagnostic and therapeutic procedure, making it an excellent tool for orthopedic surgeons like Dr. Chetan Patil S, known for offering the best arthroscopy in Bangalore.

Arthroscopy Procedure: What to Expect
1. Consultation & Diagnosis
  • Detailed discussion of symptoms and history
  • Physical examination and joint movement assessment
  • Diagnostic imaging (X-ray, MRI, CT) to evaluate internal joint damage
2. Surgical Preparation
  • Pre-operative evaluations (blood tests, imaging)
  • Patient education on procedure and recovery
  • Surgery is typically performed under regional or general anesthesia
3. The Arthroscopy Process
  • Small incisions (portals) are made around the joint
  • Arthroscope is inserted to visualize the joint
  • Surgical tools are used to remove or repair damaged tissue
  • Saline fluid is used to expand the joint and clear debris
  • Procedure time: 30 minutes to 2 hours, depending on complexity
4. Post-Procedure Care
  • Recovery begins the same day or next day
  • Pain management with medications and cold therapy
  • Use of braces or crutches (depending on joint operated)
Recovery and Rehabilitation After Arthroscopy

Recovery after arthroscopy is faster than traditional surgery, but it still requires proper care and follow-up:

First Few Days Post-Surgery:
  • Rest and elevation of the joint
  • Ice therapy to reduce swelling
  • Pain medications and antibiotics (if prescribed)
1 to 2 Weeks:
  • Wound care and suture removal
  • Gradual joint movement encouraged
  • Return to light daily activities
2 to 6 Weeks:
  • Physiotherapy to improve strength, flexibility, and function
  • Return to desk jobs or sedentary work
  • Continued reduction in swelling and stiffness
6 Weeks to 3 Months:
  • Resumption of normal activities
  • Gradual return to sports or physical tasks (if cleared by surgeon)
  • Full recovery depends on the type of procedure and patient compliance

When Should You Consider Arthroscopy?

Arthroscopy is ideal when:

  • Conservative treatment fails (medication, therapy, injections)
  • Persistent joint pain limits your mobility
  • Imaging suggests internal joint damage
  • You’re experiencing recurrent dislocations or instability
  • You need an accurate diagnosis of joint issues

Delaying treatment can worsen joint damage, so early intervention is key to preserving joint function and preventing arthritis.
