What is Ankle Surgery?

Ankle surgery involves a range of procedures aimed at repairing or reconstructing the bones, ligaments, tendons, or cartilage in the ankle joint. It may be required when non-surgical treatments like medication, rest, or physiotherapy no longer provide relief.
The ankle joint is complex and includes the tibia, fibula, and talus bones. Damage to any part of this system‚Äîdue to injury, arthritis, or deformity‚Äîcan lead to pain, instability, swelling, and difficulty walking.

Who Needs Ankle Surgery?

You may be a candidate for ankle surgery if you experience:

  • Persistent ankle pain despite conservative treatment
  • Recurrent ankle sprains or instability
  • Severe arthritis or deformity in the ankle joint
  • Fractures that didn‚Äôt heal properly (malunion or nonunion)
  • Difficulty bearing weight or walking normally
  • Torn ligaments or tendons causing mechanical problems

Common causes include:

  • Sports injuries
  • Ankle fractures
  • Rheumatoid or osteoarthritis
  • Flat foot deformity
  • Post-traumatic arthritis
  • Torn Achilles tendon or other tendinopathies
Types of Ankle Surgeries

Depending on the diagnosis, Dr. Chetan Patil S may recommend one of the following procedures:

  • ‚úÖ Ankle Arthroscopy
    A minimally invasive procedure using a small camera and instruments to diagnose and treat:
    • Loose bodies (bone or cartilage fragments)
    • Inflamed tissue
    • Ankle impingement
    • Synovitis
    • Early arthritis
  • ‚úÖ Ankle Ligament Reconstruction
    Used to treat chronic instability caused by repeated sprains. Torn or stretched ligaments are tightened or reconstructed to restore ankle strength.
  • ‚úÖ Ankle Fracture Fixation
    Broken bones in the ankle are realigned and fixed using metal plates, screws, or rods. Early treatment ensures proper healing and prevents long-term disability.
  • ‚úÖ Tendon Repair or Transfer
    In cases of Achilles tendon rupture or other tendon injuries, the damaged tendon is either repaired or replaced using nearby tissue.
  • ‚úÖ Ankle Fusion (Arthrodesis)
    For advanced arthritis, the bones of the ankle joint are fused together to eliminate pain and prevent movement that causes discomfort.
  • ‚úÖ Total Ankle Replacement
    An artificial implant replaces the damaged joint, preserving motion while relieving pain. Suitable for selected cases of advanced arthritis.
