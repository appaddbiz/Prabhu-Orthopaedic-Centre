Restore Strength and Stability with Expert Orthopaedic Care

The shoulder is one of the most mobile joints in the human body, allowing you to lift, rotate, and reach in multiple directions. However, with its mobility comes vulnerability. Injuries, degeneration, and wear-and-tear can lead to pain, stiffness, and loss of movement. When conservative treatments no longer help, shoulder surgery becomes a reliable solution for lasting relief and function. What is Shoulder Surgery?

Shoulder surgery involves surgical procedures that address problems in the bones, tendons, ligaments, and cartilage around the shoulder joint. It may be done to repair damaged tissues, realign bones, or replace parts of the joint with prosthetics. Most shoulder surgeries are now performed using arthroscopy — a minimally invasive technique using a small camera and instruments through tiny incisions, resulting in less pain and quicker recovery.

Common Conditions That May Require Shoulder Surgery
  • Rotator cuff tears
  • Recurrent shoulder dislocations
  • Frozen shoulder (adhesive capsulitis)
  • Labral tears (SLAP lesions)
  • Shoulder impingement syndrome
  • Shoulder arthritis
  • Fractures involving the shoulder blade, collarbone, or humerus
  • Post-traumatic shoulder instability

If left untreated, these conditions may worsen, causing chronic pain and affecting your ability to lift your arm, dress, or even sleep.

Types of Shoulder Surgery
  • ✅ Shoulder Arthroscopy: A keyhole procedure to treat soft tissue injuries like:
    • Rotator cuff repair
    • Labral tear repair
    • Removal of inflamed tissue or bone spurs
    • Tendon release for frozen shoulder
  • ✅ Rotator Cuff Repair: This surgery reattaches torn tendons in the shoulder, restoring strength and function.
  • ✅ Shoulder Stabilization Surgery: Done to prevent frequent dislocations by tightening loose ligaments.
  • ✅ Total Shoulder Replacement: The damaged ball-and-socket joint is replaced with artificial implants. Ideal for severe arthritis or bone loss.
  • ✅ Reverse Shoulder Replacement: Used when the rotator cuff is completely torn and not repairable. It changes the joint’s mechanics to allow better movement.
  • ✅ Fracture Fixation or Reconstruction: For shoulder fractures, plates, screws, or pins may be used to restore bone alignment.

The Treatment Process: What to Expect
  • 1. Initial Assessment:

    Dr. Chetan will review your medical history, conduct a physical exam, and may order imaging tests (X-ray, MRI, or CT scan) to evaluate the damage.

  • 2. Non-Surgical Options First:

    Before recommending surgery, Dr. Chetan may suggest:

    • Anti-inflammatory medications
    • Physiotherapy
    • Shoulder injections (like corticosteroids)
    • Activity modifications

    If these fail to provide relief, surgical intervention will be considered.

  • 3. Surgical Procedure:

    Depending on the diagnosis, Dr. Chetan will perform the appropriate surgery — either arthroscopically or via open procedure. Most arthroscopic surgeries take 1–2 hours, are performed under regional or general anesthesia, and require minimal hospital stay.

Recovery and Rehabilitation After Shoulder Surgery

Recovery timelines vary depending on the type of surgery, age, and patient condition. However, with Dr. Chetan’s structured rehabilitation protocol, most patients regain excellent shoulder function.

🏥 Immediate Post-Surgery Care
  • Use of a shoulder sling for comfort and support
  • Pain management and wound care
  • Initiation of passive motion exercises in early days (as advised)
💪 Physical Therapy
  • Gradual progression from passive to active-assisted and then active exercises
  • Focus on restoring range of motion, strength, and stability
🕒 Recovery Time:
  • Minor procedures: 2–4 weeks
  • Rotator cuff repair: 8–12 weeks
  • Shoulder replacement: 3–6 months
🏃‍♂️ Return to Activities
  • Light daily tasks: 3–4 weeks
  • Driving: 6–8 weeks
  • Sports: 3–6 months, depending on surgery type and progress

Dr. Chetan closely monitors each patient’s recovery with personalized follow-ups to ensure safe, effective healing.
