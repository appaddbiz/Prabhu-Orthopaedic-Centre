Advanced Minimally Invasive Surgery for Shoulder Disorders

The shoulder joint is one of the most flexible and complex joints in the body, enabling a wide range of arm movements essential for daily activities and sports. However, injuries, wear and tear, or inflammation can cause shoulder pain, stiffness, and limited function. Shoulder arthroscopy is a cutting-edge minimally invasive surgical procedure that allows precise diagnosis and treatment of various shoulder conditions.

For those seeking the best shoulder arthroscopy in Bangalore, expert orthopedic surgeons like Dr. Chetan Patil S offer comprehensive care using the latest arthroscopic techniques and personalized treatment plans to restore shoulder health effectively.

What is Shoulder Arthroscopy?

Shoulder arthroscopy is a surgical procedure where a small camera called an arthroscope is inserted through tiny incisions around the shoulder joint. This allows the surgeon to visualize the inside of the joint on a monitor and perform surgical repairs using specialized instruments, all without the need for large open incisions.

This minimally invasive approach helps treat a variety of shoulder problems with less tissue damage, reduced pain, and quicker recovery compared to traditional open surgery.

Common Conditions Treated with Shoulder Arthroscopy
  • Rotator Cuff Tears: Partial or complete tears of the tendons that stabilize the shoulder
  • Shoulder Impingement Syndrome: Compression of the rotator cuff tendons causing pain and inflammation
  • Labral Tears: Injury to the cartilage rim around the shoulder socket (glenoid labrum) causing instability
  • Frozen Shoulder (Adhesive Capsulitis): Tightening and thickening of the shoulder capsule causing stiffness
  • Shoulder Instability and Dislocations: Repair of torn ligaments or capsular tissues to stabilize the joint
  • Biceps Tendon Injuries: Inflammation, tears, or ruptures of the biceps tendon
  • Removal of Bone Spurs or Loose Bodies: To alleviate pain and improve movement
  • Arthritis Treatment: Debridement or joint smoothing procedures in early arthritis

How is Shoulder Arthroscopy Performed?
Preoperative Preparation
  • Complete assessment including imaging tests
  • Instructions on fasting and medication adjustments prior to surgery
  • Administration of general or regional anesthesia
Surgical Procedure
  • The patient is positioned typically in a beach chair or lateral decubitus position
  • Several small incisions (portals) around the shoulder are made, usually 3-4 incisions about 0.5 to 1 cm in size
  • The arthroscope is inserted to visualize the joint interior, and sterile fluid is infused to expand the joint space
  • Specialized instruments are inserted through the other portals to repair damaged tissues or remove unwanted structures
  • The procedure duration ranges from 45 minutes to 2 hours based on complexity
  • Incisions are closed with sutures or adhesive strips and dressed
Recovery and Rehabilitation
Immediately After Surgery
  • Mild to moderate pain and swelling are managed with prescribed pain medications and cold compresses
  • The arm is usually immobilized in a sling for a few days to weeks depending on the surgery
  • Early gentle movements may be encouraged to prevent stiffness
Initial Weeks
  • Supervised physiotherapy starts with passive and assisted range of motion exercises
  • Gradual weaning off the sling as mobility improves
  • Controlled strengthening exercises begin to rebuild shoulder muscles
Long-Term Rehabilitation
  • Progressive strengthening and functional exercises to restore shoulder stability and motion
  • Return to daily activities generally within 6 to 12 weeks
  • Resuming sports or heavy physical work after 4 to 6 months depending on recovery progress
Benefits of Shoulder Arthroscopy
  • Minimally invasive with small incisions and reduced tissue trauma
  • Lower risk of infection and complications compared to open surgery
  • Shorter hospital stay, often outpatient or one-day surgery
  • Faster recovery and less postoperative pain
  • Precise diagnosis and simultaneous treatment of shoulder problems
  • Better cosmetic results with minimal scarring

  • Adherence to rehabilitation and physical therapy
  • Overall health and lifestyle factors

Who is a Candidate for Shoulder Arthroscopy?

You may be a candidate if you have:

  • Persistent shoulder pain or dysfunction despite conservative treatments
  • Diagnosed rotator cuff tears, labral injuries, or shoulder instability
  • Mechanical symptoms such as catching, clicking, or weakness
  • Early arthritis requiring joint debridement or smoothing
  • Need for diagnostic evaluation when imaging is inconclusive
