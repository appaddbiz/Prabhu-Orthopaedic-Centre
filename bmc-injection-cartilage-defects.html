Bone Marrow Concentrate Injection for Cartilage Defects: A Regenerative Solution

Cartilage damage, whether from injury, wear and tear, or degeneration, can cause persistent joint pain, stiffness, and limited mobility. Traditional surgical methods for cartilage repair often involve long recovery periods and inconsistent outcomes. Today, Bone Marrow Concentrate (BMC) Injection offers a minimally invasive and highly effective regenerative solution.

Understanding Cartilage Defects

Cartilage is the smooth, rubbery tissue that cushions joints and allows smooth movement. Unlike other tissues in the body, cartilage has a limited capacity to heal on its own due to the lack of blood vessels.

Causes of Cartilage Damage:

  • Sports injuries (especially to the knee, hip, ankle, or shoulder)
  • Degenerative diseases like osteoarthritis
  • Repetitive joint stress or trauma
  • Congenital abnormalities

Symptoms:

  • Pain during joint movement
  • Swelling and stiffness
  • Joint instability
  • Clicking or grinding sensation
What is Bone Marrow Concentrate (BMC) Therapy?

Bone Marrow Concentrate Injection is an advanced regenerative treatment that utilizes your body’s own stem cells and growth factors to heal and regenerate damaged cartilage.

The Science Behind It:

Bone marrow contains mesenchymal stem cells (MSCs)—cells capable of developing into cartilage, bone, and other connective tissues. When concentrated and injected into the site of damage, these cells:

  • Promote cartilage regeneration
  • Stimulate tissue repair
  • Reduce inflammation and pain

This technique offers a natural healing process without the need for synthetic implants or invasive surgeries.

Benefits of BMC Therapy for Cartilage Defects
  • ✔️ Minimally Invasive – No open surgery or long incisions
  • ✔️ Quick Recovery – Faster return to daily activities and sports
  • ✔️ Natural Repair – Harnesses your body’s own healing capacity
  • ✔️ Pain Reduction – Significant relief from chronic joint pain
  • ✔️ Joint Preservation – Delays or eliminates the need for joint replacement

It is especially ideal for younger patients or active individuals looking to maintain an active lifestyle without the risks associated with major surgery.

Conditions Treated with BMC for Cartilage Defects
  • ✅ Knee cartilage lesions (from sports or trauma)
  • ✅ Early osteoarthritis
  • ✅ Cartilage wear in the hip or shoulder
  • ✅ Ankle or elbow cartilage injuries
  • ✅ Post-meniscectomy cartilage thinning

At Prabhu Orthopaedic Centre, Dr. Chetan Patil S carefully evaluates each patient to determine whether BMC therapy is appropriate and likely to yield long-term benefits.

The Procedure: Step-by-Step

1. Consultation and Imaging

  • An initial evaluation includes a thorough physical examination, patient history, and imaging such as MRI to assess the extent of cartilage damage.

2. Bone Marrow Harvest

  • Performed under local anesthesia
  • Bone marrow is extracted from the pelvic (iliac) bone using a specialized needle
  • Safe, quick, and usually completed in 20–30 minutes

3. Processing the Concentrate

  • The bone marrow is spun in a centrifuge to isolate a rich concentrate of stem cells and healing factors

4. Injection

  • The BMC is injected directly into the damaged joint
  • Image guidance (ultrasound or fluoroscopy) ensures precise placement

5. Recovery

  • Mild soreness at the extraction or injection site is normal
  • Most patients resume light activities within a few days
  • Physical therapy is typically recommended to support recovery
Expected Results and Recovery

Most patients begin to experience relief from symptoms within 2–6 weeks post-procedure. Over the following months, regenerative effects continue to improve:

  • Joint function
  • Pain levels
  • Cartilage thickness (as shown on follow-up imaging)

Success depends on:

  • Extent of cartilage damage
  • Stage of osteoarthritis
  • Compliance with rehabilitation
