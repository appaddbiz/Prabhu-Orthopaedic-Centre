Regaining Mobility and Relieving Chronic Pain

The foot is a highly complex structure made up of 26 bones, 33 joints, and over 100 muscles, tendons, and ligaments. It supports our body weight, enables motion, and absorbs the impact during movement. When foot problems arise—whether due to injury, deformity, arthritis, or other medical conditions—they can severely impair your quality of life. In such cases, Foot Surgery may be the most effective option to restore functionality and relieve pain.

If you're seeking expert care for your foot condition, consulting with Dr. Chetan Patil S, known for offering the best foot surgery in Bangalore, can ensure you receive high-quality treatment with excellent outcomes.

What is Foot Surgery?

Foot surgery involves various surgical techniques to correct structural abnormalities, repair injuries, remove deformities, or treat chronic conditions affecting the bones, joints, tendons, ligaments, or nerves in the foot.

Surgery may be required when:

  • Conservative treatments fail (e.g., medications, physical therapy, orthotics)
  • There is a structural deformity like bunions or flat feet
  • Severe arthritis or joint damage is present
  • There are fractures or trauma-related injuries
  • Nerve compression or tumors exist in the foot
  • Chronic conditions like plantar fasciitis or Achilles tendinitis do not improve

With modern advancements, many foot surgeries are now minimally invasive, offering quicker recovery, smaller scars, and fewer complications.

Common Types of Foot Surgery

Here are some of the most common foot procedures performed by orthopedic specialists:

  • Bunion Surgery (Bunionectomy)
    Removes the bony bump at the base of the big toe and realigns the joint to relieve pain and restore shape.
  • Flat Foot Reconstruction
    Corrects collapsed arches, often through tendon repair, osteotomy (bone cutting and realignment), or fusion of joints.
  • Plantar Fasciitis Release
    For chronic heel pain unresponsive to other treatments, the plantar fascia ligament is partially cut to relieve tension and inflammation.
  • Arthritis Surgery
    Joint fusion (arthrodesis) or joint replacement may be used to treat foot arthritis in the midfoot, hindfoot, or toes.
  • Neuroma Surgery
    Removes thickened nerve tissue (e.g., Morton’s neuroma) causing tingling, burning, or numbness in the ball of the foot.
  • Heel Spur Removal
    Surgery to remove calcium deposits (heel spurs) that cause pain when walking or standing.
  • Tendon Repair
    For ruptured or degenerated tendons like the Achilles tendon, surgical repair or tendon transfer may be necessary.
  • Foot Fracture Fixation
    For displaced or non-healing fractures, surgery with screws, plates, or pins ensures proper bone healing and alignment.
Treatment Process
1. Evaluation and Diagnosis
  • Detailed medical history and physical examination
  • Imaging tests: X-ray, MRI, CT scan, or ultrasound
  • Gait and pressure analysis for biomechanical insight
2. Conservative Management (If Applicable)
  • Medications (anti-inflammatory drugs, steroids)
  • Physiotherapy and orthotic devices
  • Rest, ice, compression, and elevation (RICE)
  • Injections for pain relief
3. Surgical Planning

When non-surgical methods fail, your orthopedic surgeon will recommend an appropriate foot surgery, tailored to your diagnosis and lifestyle needs.

Recovery and Rehabilitation After Foot Surgery

Recovery after foot surgery varies depending on the procedure and your overall health.

Initial Recovery (0–2 Weeks)
  • Pain and swelling managed with medications and elevation
  • Use of surgical shoes, cast, or splints to immobilize the foot
  • Non-weight-bearing or partial weight-bearing (crutches or walker)
Early Rehab (2–6 Weeks)
  • Sutures and dressings removed
  • Gradual increase in mobility
  • Physical therapy begins (range of motion, strengthening)
Full Recovery (6–12 Weeks or More)
  • Transition to full weight-bearing
  • Continued physiotherapy
  • Return to daily activities and sports as guided

The timeline for full healing can range from 2 to 6 months based on the complexity of the surgery. Compliance with post-operative care is crucial for the best foot surgery in Bangalore results.
