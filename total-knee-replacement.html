What Is Total Knee Replacement?

Total Knee Replacement (TKR)â€”also called knee arthroplastyâ€”is a surgical procedure that involves replacing a damaged or diseased knee joint with an artificial implant (prosthesis). The damaged parts of the femur (thigh bone), tibia (shin bone), and sometimes the patella (kneecap) are removed and replaced with high-quality metal and plastic components.

When Is Total Knee Replacement Needed?

You may be a candidate for Total Knee Replacement if you experience:

  • Severe knee pain during walking, climbing stairs, or even resting
  • Swelling, stiffness, and limited range of motion
  • Bowed legs or knee deformity
  • Pain that does not improve with medication, physiotherapy, or injections
  • Advanced arthritis visible in X-rays or MRI
Conditions Treated by Total Knee Replacement
  • Osteoarthritis (most common reason for TKR)
  • Rheumatoid Arthritis
  • Post-traumatic Arthritis
  • Knee Deformities (bow legs or knock knees)
  • Knee Joint Damage due to Injury

The Total Knee Replacement Procedure
  • Anesthesia: Either general or spinal anesthesia is administered.
  • Incision: A small incision is made to expose the knee joint.
  • Resurfacing: The damaged cartilage and bone are removed.
  • Implant Placement: Artificial components are precisely fitted to recreate joint function.
  • Closure: The incision is closed, and a sterile dressing is applied.

The entire procedure typically takes 1.5 to 2 hours.
