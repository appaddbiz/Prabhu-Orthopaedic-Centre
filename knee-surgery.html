What is Knee Surgery?

Knee surgery involves repairing or replacing parts of the knee joint to relieve pain and improve function. It is often recommended when conservative treatments like medication, injections, and physiotherapy no longer work.

Common Types of Knee Surgery Include:

  • Total Knee Replacement (TKR): Replacement of the entire knee joint with artificial components.
  • Partial Knee Replacement (PKR): Only the damaged part of the knee is replaced.
  • Knee Arthroscopy: A minimally invasive procedure to diagnose and treat problems like meniscal tears, ligament injuries, and cartilage damage.
  • Ligament Reconstruction (ACL, PCL): Common in athletes, this surgery restores stability after ligament injury.
  • Revision Knee Surgery: Performed if a previous knee replacement has worn out or developed complications.
Who Needs Knee Surgery?

You may need knee surgery if you experience:

  • Persistent pain, swelling, or stiffness in the knee
  • Difficulty walking, climbing stairs, or bending the joint
  • Knee deformity (bow legs or knock knees)
  • Loss of mobility and quality of life
  • Ineffectiveness of non-surgical treatments

Common conditions treated with knee surgery include:

  • Osteoarthritis
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Post-traumatic arthritis
  • Meniscal or ligament injuries
  • Fractures around the knee joint

How is Knee Surgery Performed?

The surgical procedure varies based on the condition being treated. Here’s a look at the most common knee surgery—Total Knee Replacement (TKR):

  • Pre-Surgical Preparation:
    • Assessment through X-rays, blood tests, and fitness evaluation
    • Pre-surgery counseling to help you understand the procedure and recovery
  • During Surgery:
    • Performed under spinal or general anesthesia
    • Damaged cartilage and bone are removed
    • Artificial implants (made of metal and plastic) are fixed to recreate the knee joint
    • The procedure lasts about 1.5 to 2 hours
  • Post-Surgery Hospital Stay:
    • Usually 2–3 days, with pain management and physical therapy initiated early
Recovery and Rehabilitation

Recovery is a key part of the surgical success. Dr. Chetan provides a customized recovery plan based on your condition, age, and lifestyle.

  • Early Recovery:
    • Start walking with support within 24–48 hours
    • Pain is controlled with medications and therapy
    • Exercises to strengthen muscles begin under guidance
  • Continued Rehabilitation:
    • Home-based or outpatient physiotherapy
    • Improvement in range of motion and mobility
    • Gradual return to normal activities within 6–8 weeks
  • Full Recovery:
    • Light exercises like swimming or walking by 3 months
    • Most patients return to normal life and even sports in 4–6 months
    • Knee implants can last 15–20 years or more
